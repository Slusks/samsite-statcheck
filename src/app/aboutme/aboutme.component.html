About Me

Problem Solving Engineer and Data Enthusiast

Hi, my name is Sam! I am an Aeronautical and Mechanical engineering graduate of Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute who has spent the majority of my career since graduation working as a Base Support Engineer at American Airlines.

As an engineer at American, my day to day work involved designing structural repairs for aircraft, interpreting and communicating manufacturer details to production staff, and managing the engineering project requirements for up to 3 aircraft at a time. In addition to these responsibilities, I was selected for several process improvement projects at American. I was the lead engineer tasked with redefining the aircraft damage log procedures as well as the procedures used after a large hail or lightning storm. I worked as part of a team exploring drone technology and developing a business case to apply it to American Airlines maintenance and inspection program.

My experiences developing solutions to systemic problems as an engineer has led me to a personal interest in programming and data analytics. I have been working to grow my proficiency with Python, SQL, and Tableau in order to improve my ability to gather data and communicate insights in a professional setting.
